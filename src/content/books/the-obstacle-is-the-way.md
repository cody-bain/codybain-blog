Somehow I completed my entire education without ever reading *The Odyssey*, so I figured it was due time I paid my dues.

Wasn't a huge fan of the early chapters until Odysseus reached the Phaeacians and began to tell his tragic tale in full. The text, while elegant, was drawn out and repetitive. Some nights I only managed a page or two before falling asleep.

Nothing in particular *resonated* with me, but I appreciated how each group Odysseus visited took pride in a clearly defined cultural identity, like the Phaeacians and their sailing prowess. It makes me nostalgic for a world I never knew.

Overall, I did enjoy the book, it just took some time for me to actually get engaged. For an 8th century BC Greek text, I'm impressed with the richness of storytelling in this return-home adventure.
